World Hunger: The Global EMERGENCY

The Prevalence of World Hunger

World hunger has been an ongoing prevalent issue globally for centuries. Many people struggle to survive due to a lack of food and water. In 2023, the number of people affected by world hunger has increased to 333 million people. Conflict only rises that number, with areas affected by war and violence containing 70% of the world's hungry people. Additionally, food as well as global fertilizer prices have increased substantially, especially over these past few years. As these prices rise, food insecurity will increase.

Consequences of World Hunger

World hunger has many consequences that can affect people's lives. Some of these include:

  • Lack of access to clean water
  • 783 million people going to bed hungry each night
  • Lack of access to basic nutrition
  • Malnutrient deficiency
  • Stunted growth (in children)
The list goes on with these consequences, but all of these are serious issues that can affect people's lives.
